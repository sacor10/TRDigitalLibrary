For all the public roles he played — soldier, governor, president — Theodore
Roosevelt's letters to his family reveal a strikingly tender private man. Writing
to his son Kermit and his wife Edith, he traded the cadences of the platform for
jokes, drawings, and the small news of daily life.

## Letters across distance

Roosevelt wrote constantly, even from the field and from the White House. His
"picture letters" to his children — illustrated, playful, attentive — are among
the most beloved documents he left behind. They show a father determined to stay
present in his children's lives no matter how far his duties carried him.

## The private Roosevelt

These letters complicate the larger-than-life caricature. Beside the trust-buster
and the Rough Rider stands a man anxious about a child's health, delighted by a
pet, eager for news from home. Read together, they are a quiet companion to the
public record.
